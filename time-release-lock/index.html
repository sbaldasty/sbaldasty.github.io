Time release lock
2023-08-05

I used to eat way too much junk food, and I didn't care. I was young and active enough so that whatever I wanted to eat didn't seem to affect me. Then all that changed: too much ice cream or chocolate began to negatively affect my mood, and my weight too, but I couldn't seem to control myself even as the stakes grew. Thankfully life hacks can go a long way towards making up for gaps in willpower. I eventually learned about commitment devices, and one in particular solved the problem for me.

The central piece of the solution is a special kind of lock called a time release lock. A time release lock has no keyhole or combination mechanism; instead it has a timer you can set. When you close the lock and start the timer, the lock only opens again when the timer reaches zero. The other piece of the solution is a container for storing junk food in. Any container with a hole for the shackle of the lock to slip through will do, but I use a large container because I can save money by taking advantage of bulk deals on my favorite junk food and still have adequate space for it all. I use a repurposed toolbox.

In the morning, I enjoy choosing a reasonable amount of junk food from the container to last me the day. I give myself plenty of leeway about what reasonable means. I only want to avoid overindulgence, not punish myself. After that I make my commitment by locking the container for slightly less than 24 hours, so it opens again the next morning (it's just a couple button presses because the lock conveniently reuses the previous time setting).

I allow myself to eat my junk food any time I want, but I usually eat some after breakfast and the rest after lunch. If I experience cravings after that, I sometimes feel upset that the rest my junk food is locked up. I take a little time to remember how my past self limited the choices I have now because he cared about me, and how in the morning I will get to choose more junk food and then make the same commitment all over again, limiting the choices my future self will have because I care about him.

About restocking the container with junk food, I fill it to the very top so I have to restock less frequently. On restocking days I have access to whatever junk food I buy until the lock opens, so minimizing how often that happens is best. Sometimes I shop in the early morning before locking the container, and sometimes I just accept the possibility that I may overeat that day.

The lock requires charging through a Type-B Micro USB port every couple months
