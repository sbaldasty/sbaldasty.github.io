The last time I saw my parents, we visited the [New Hampshire Telephone Museum](https://www.nhtelephonemuseum.org/) together. I recommend visiting the museum to anyone interested learning about the history of telegraphs and telephones from knowledgeable staff, or seeing firsthand some of the fascinating artifacts on which our communications infrastructure is built, past and present. There were also several hands-on demos: telegraphs, manual switchboards, automated switchboards, and early coin operated telephones.

Beyond that, I had a special reason to take interest in the Telephone Museum. I [posted recently](/posts/information-theory/) on a class I took about Information Theory that I took, and how one of the supplemental readings told through countless anecdotes how information-related technologies changed the world many times over. Well naturally, the exhibits on display in this museum bring to life a wide and important swath of that history. There is even a whole room dedicated to the life of Alexander Bell.
