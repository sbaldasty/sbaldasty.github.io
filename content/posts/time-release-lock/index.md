I used to eat way too much junkfood, and I didn't care. I was young and active enough so that whatever I wanted to eat didn't seem to affect me. Then all that changed: too much ice cream or chocolate began to negatively affect my mood, and my weight too, but I couldn't seem to control myself even as the stakes grew. Thankfully life hacks can go a long way towards making up for gaps in willpower. I eventually learned about [commitment devices](https://medium.com/behavior-design-hub/your-commitment-devices-database-35a54df3a64f), and one in particular solved the problem for me.

The central piece of the solution is a special kind of lock called a [time release lock](https://www.amazon.com/Display-Multi-Function-Electronic-Rechargeable-Padlock/dp/B09D7RC5WX). A time release lock has no keyhole or combination mechanism; instead it has a timer you can set. When you close the lock and start the timer, the lock only opens again when the timer reaches zero. The other piece of the solution is a container for storing junkfood in. Any container with a hole for the shackle of the lock to slip through will do, but I use a large container because I can save money by taking advantage of bulk deals on my favorite junkfood and still have adequate space for it all. I use a repurposed toolbox.

In the morning, I enjoy choosing a reasonable amount of junkfood from the container to last me the day. I give myself plenty of leeway about what _reasonable_ means. I only want to avoid overindulgence, not punish myself. After that I make my commitment by locking the container for slightly less than 24 hours, so it opens again the next morning (it's just a couple button presses because the lock conveniently reuses the previous time setting).

I allow myself to eat my junkfood any time I want, but I usually eat some after breakfast and the rest after lunch. If I experience cravings after that, I sometimes feel upset that the rest my junkfood is locked up. I take a little time to remember how my past self limited the choices I have now because he cared about me, and how in the morning I will get to choose more junkfood and then make the same commitment all over again, limiting the choices my future self will have because I care about him.

About restocking the container with junkfood, I fill it to the very top so I have to restock less frequently. On restocking days I have access to whatever junkfood I buy until the lock opens, so minimizing how often that happens is best. Sometimes I shop in the early morning before locking the container, and sometimes I just accept the possibility that I may overeat that day.
