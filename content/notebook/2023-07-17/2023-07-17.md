## Performance of mpyc stats mean

Stats mean works well on integers but cannot be used for two reasons.

- Like regular integer division, statistics.mean cannot be composed with `max`.

- Since we want to keep secret which parties are participating in the mean, we'd have to represent their contributions as a `seclist` which is not compatible with `stats.mean`.

## Using gcd instead of division

No reason to believe it won't work as described, but I haven't implemented it yet.

## Parties sometimes choose to split  but choose the same for both sides

I'm pretty sure it's a normal thing that can happen with our protocol, not a bug. I think the solution is to reduce the redundant splits out of the model after the fact in the clear.

## Running insecure mpyc as a single process

Turned out to be impractical because controlling the inputs from the parties was not compatible with the goal of being able to use without modification.

## Running insecure mpyc as multiple processes

No reason to believe this isn't possible but it's a heavy lift. Another issue with investing in a fast insecure version of mpyc is that mpyc is still under active development.
