---
title: Remote work training takeaways
---

_I took notes from an online class I took about remote work. I think the class was on LinkedIn._

Identify which tasks absolutely have to be done today. What remains is discretionary time. Have a space dedicated to work. Consider natural light, sound, and temperatures.

Do not let non-work activities be an option during work time:
- No Personal calls or messages
- Keep distractions out of sight
- Remember breakfast, remember lunch, but don't snack; it's a distraction
- Don't sit by a window

Form a solid routine for the day:
- Start at a consistent time
- Maintain a regular emails / communication cadence
- Choose actions thoughtfully instead of randomly
- Be flexible because not every day is average
- Start day with 5 minutes of planning
- Intentionally break routines for added creative thought
- Shake it up when you can't get focused

Choices about how to monitor electronic connections (professional channels, not personal ones):
- Form specific rule about when and how to use
- Push out information that could be useful to others
- Send out status updates often (tell relevent people exactly where you stand)
- Prefer phone and video calls when possible

Start conversations with a positive tone. End with expressing willingness to talk further. Face to face, video is best. Acknowledge when you receive feedback and state how you will use it. Useful feedback is:
- Specific not general
- Descriptive, not evaluative - make people aware of issues, don't condemn people
- Often prefaced with phrases like _"I feel..."_ or _"My observation is..."_
- About issues, not people (performance is independent of people performing)
- Timely (faster is better)
- Two-way, open for a dialog

Tactics for feeling connected, focused, part of the team:
- Find ways to connect "in person" on a "regular basis"
- Remember the higher purpose you serve - think about it for a minute regularly
- Use technology and tools to socialize a little, about 5% of your workday - it makes you human to others
- Up to 10% of your workday should be used "not working", Just taking breaks, connecting with others - you'll become human again, more willing and able to focus on your work

About politics:
- Maintain relationship with boss - how you're doing, projects, socialize, etc.
- Maintain relationships with team members
- Weigh in on important decisions that happen; but pick what's important
- Maintain a bigger dialog with a few select people

Ideally have a private room. Building rapport requires more intentionality. Say hi. Ask for help. Get help. Avoid doing chores during work breaks. Bring your whole self to work. Show what kind of person you are, with pictures or other expression.

_"This is still work, and you need to treat it as such."_

_"Feeling distracted happens to everyone. At home you have more options."_
